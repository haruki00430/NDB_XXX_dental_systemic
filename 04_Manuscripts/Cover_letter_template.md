**Summary of the study.** Using prefecture-level open data from Japan’s National Database of Health Insurance Claims and Specific Health Checkups (NDB No.10, fiscal year 2023), we examined whether periodontal disease claim burden is ecologically associated with surrogate indicators of diabetic complication management across 47 prefectures. We report a hypothesis-generating positive association with diabetic complication management (B001-20) alongside null findings for HbA1c-based metrics, supported by negative control (caries) and sensitivity analyses including spatial diagnostics.

**Fit for the journal.** We believe this work will interest readers of [*Journal name*] because it links oral and diabetes-related claims at a population level in Japan, a setting with high dialysis burden, and explicitly discusses limitations of ecological inference and checkup participation bias.
